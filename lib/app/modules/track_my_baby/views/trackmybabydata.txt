What is Jaundice?
Jaundice is yellowing of skin and eyes caused by a high level of bilirubin in the baby blood. It is very common in newborn and usually appear within 2-4 days after birth.

Why does it happen?
Newborn have immature lives that cut break down bilirubin quickly.
It is usually harmless and goes away on its own.
Premature babies or babies with blood group differences may have higher risk.

Signs to look for
Yellows colors starting from face and moving to chest & limbs.
Yellow eyes (Sclera)
Baby seem sleepier than usual
Trouble Feeding
Less active or not waking for feeds.

When to worry
	Contact a doctor immediately if:
Yellow color spreads to legs, hands, palm.
Baby is very sleepy, not feeding well
Baby is vomiting and has high pitch cry
Jaundice appears within 24hrs of birth

What can you do at home
Frequent feeding (2-3 hrs.) helps nemone bilirubin through stool.
Sunlight exposure : undress baby and place near indirect morning sunlight for 10-15 min, 2-3 times a day (Never Mid day sun)
Keep baby Hydrated
