Week-by-week overview   Birth....1 year


Weeks 1—4 (Newborn	1 month)


Typical changes in the first month: reflexive movement, sleep lots, bonding, early social smiles start near end of month.


Week 1



*	Motor: mostly reflexes (rooting, sucking, Moro/startle); holds limbs flexed.
*	Social/Communication: cries to signal needs; calmed by caregiver voice; briefly fixes gaze on face- like patterns.
*	Feeding/Sleep: feeds frequently(breast or bottle) every 2—4 hours; long sleep periods but wakes to
feed.



Week 2



*	Motor: smoother sucking; may lift head briefly when prone.
*	Social: begins to show brief, fleeting social smile (some newborns start later).
*	Sensory: tracks slow moving objects briefly.



Week 3



*	Motor: stronger hands, opens fingers more.
*	Communication: cooing sounds may appear; quieter when spoken to.

*	Feeding/Sleep: more organized feeding and slightly longer awake windows.



Week 4 (=1 month)
 
*	Motor: turns head to sound; can briefly lift head when on tummy.

*	Social: social smile may appear (many babies smile socially by 4—6 weeks).
*	Sensory: follows objects across midline.







Weeks 5-8 (-2 months)



Major milestones: more purposeful movements, social smiling, gurgling/cooing, improved head control.


Week 5



*	Motor: better head control when upright; fewer floppy moments.

*	Communication: cooing and gurgling more frequent.
*	Social: enjoys face-to-face interaction; may follow faces.


Week 6


*	Motor: holds head up more steadily during tummy time.

*	Social: smiles reliably in response to faces “social smile" clearer.

*	Sensory: tracks objects further and shiRs gaze.



Week 7



*	Communication: turns toward sounds; responds to caregiver voice with coos.

*	Motor: begins to bring hands together and to mouth.
 
Week 8 (-2 months)


*	Motor: pushes up on forearms during tummy time; begins to open hands and grasp rattle reflexively.

*	Communication: vowels (ooh/ahh), responds to play with gurgles; begins to imitate some sounds.
*	Social: enjoys shol games, recognizes caregivers.







Weeks 9-12 (-3 months)



Expect increased control: liñs chest on forearms, laughing, purposeful grasping.



Week 9


*	Motor: liñs chest higher in prone; begins to bear weight on legs when held upright.
*	Communication: more varied cooing; may laugh out loud soon.


Week 10


*	Social: responds to familiar people with joyful expressions; watches faces intently.

*	Sensory: reaches for high-contrast toys.


Week 11


*	Motor: hands begin to open more; reaches for and bats at toys.
*	Communication: begins to use longer vowel sounds and may chuckle.
 
Week 12 (-3 months)


*	Motor: holds head steady unsuppoled; pushes up on hands; may roll front	back sometimes.

*	Communication: laughs, squeals; turns to voice and shows delight.

*	Vision/hearing: tracks across 180 degrees.







Weeks 13-16 (-4 months)



Growing fine motor, purposeful reaching, rolling tendencies, social play.


Week 13


*	Motor: begins purposeful reaching; can bring objects to mouth.
*	Communication: consonant sounds appear (b, d, g-like).


Week 14


*	Motor: rolls front	back more often; sits with suppol.
*	Sociale:njoyms irror/peek-a-boointeractions.

Week 15


*	Fine motor: begins raking grasp (whole hand) to pick up toys.
*	Communication: vocal play	babbles and experiments with tone.
 
Week 16 (-4 months)



*	Motor: sits with trunk suppol; good head control; may roll both directions soon.

*	Social/Communication: laughs, responds to name sometimes; copies some mouth movements.





Weeks 17-20 (-5 months)



Sitting progress, first solid foods often begun near 6 months but some families start tastes earlier per local guidance; babbling increases.


Week 17


*	Motor: sits propped on hands for short periods; bexer midline play (hands together).

*	Communication: repetitive babbling (ba-ba, ma-ma emerging as sounds, not yet words).



Week 18


*	Sensory: explores objects with mouth and hands intentionally.
*	Social: recognizes familiar people; may show stranger hesitation.


Week 19


*	Motor: pivots on tummy, may stal to scoot; transfers toys hand-to-hand.

*	Feeding: may show readiness for solids near 6 months; consult pediatrician.



Week 20 (-5 months)
 
*	Communication: responds to name more reliably; uses varied consonanVvowel combinations.

*	Motor: sits unsuppoled briefly with trunk control improving.



Weeks 21-24 (-6 months)



Major changes: sits steady, rolls both ways, begins crawling axempts, first teeth likely; solids commonly introduced.


Week 21



*	Motor: sits unsuppoled for longer stretches.

*	Fine motor: raking grasp improving; reaches for small objects.



Week 22



*	Feeding: may accept pureed/mashed foods if advised by clinician; begins to self-feed with fingers (pick up soft foods).
*	Social: enjoys games like peek-a-boo; shows increased social back-and-fonh.



Week 23



*	Motor: may begin rocking on hands/knees in preparation for crawling.
*	Communication: babbling becomes more complex (variegated babble like “mabada").



Week 24 (-6 months)



*	Motor: may sit well unsupported and begin to pull to sit; may stan to attempt crawling/pivoting.
 
*	Sensory/cognitive: explores objects in more complex ways (shaking, banging).



Weeks 25-28 (-7 months)



Improved mobility (creeping/crawling begins), object permanence foundations, stronger pincer development stating.


Week 25



*	Motor: crawling or commando-crawlingpossible; sits steadily and rotates to reach toys.
*	Communication: uses consonant strings and responds with sounds to conversation.



Week 26



*	Fine motor: developing raking	beginning pincer (index + thumb) practice toward end of range.

*	Social: shows preference for celain people/toys; may show anxiety with strangers.



Week 27



*	Cognitive: experiments with object permanence (looks for partially hidden toy).

*	Feeding: picks up finger foods, brings to mouth.



Week 28 (-7 months)



*	Motor: may pull up to stand with support; actively moves to reach objects.

*	Communication: imitates speech sounds and intonation.
 
Weeks 29-32 (-8 months)


Pulling to stand, cruising with support begins, clearer gestures (pointing), more varied babble.



Week 29



*	Motor: pulls to stand using furniture; stands with support.
*	Fine motor: improved thumb-index opposition practice.


Week 30


*	Communication: uses gestures (reaching, pointing); responds to “no” sometimes.
*	Social: plays interactive games and shows separation anxiety stating for some.



Week 31


*	Cognitive: searches for completely hidden objects (improving object permanence).
*	Motor: may begin cruising (moving around while holding furniture).



Week 32 (-8 months)


*	Communication: understands simple words like “baby,” “bottle"; babbles with rhythm.
*	Feeding: self-feeding improves; tries variety of textures.
 
Weeks 33-36 (-9 months)



Confidence in standing/cruising; first signs of intentional pointing; may say “mama/dada” nonspecifically.


Week 33



*	Motor: cruises well, stands briefly without support possible.
*	Fine motor: pincer grasp more refined (able to pick up small pieces).



Week 34



*	Communication: may say “mama/dada” specifically for parents by end of this period for many babies.
*	Social: shows clear preferences and may show checking back to caregiver when exploring.



Week 35



*	Cognitive: imitates simple actions; explores cause/eñect (drops things to watch them fall).
*	Motor: lowers to floor from standing.



Week 36 (-9 months)



*	Communication: understands simple commands accompanied by gestures; points to share interest.
*	Mobility: may take steps holding hands.
 
Weeks 37-40 (-10 months)


Refinedmobility, more intentionalwords, play becomesmore complex (pretendactions).


Week 37


*	Motor: may stand briefly alone; improved balance while cruising.
*	Communication:increased consonantsoundsand intentional approximations of words.


Week 38


*	Social: plays simple back-and-folh games; may show separation anxiety peaks.

*	Cognitive: uses objects correctly sometimes (drinks from cup, pats doll).


Week 39


*	Fine motor: more precise pincer	picks small bits of food.

*	Communication: recognizes names of familiar people/objects.


Week 40 (-10 months)


*	Motor: may begin independent steps (some start between 9—1S months).
*	Play: points to objects to request or share interest.


Weekss 41-44 (-11 months)


Word approximations, stands and may take steps; understands more language.
 
Week 41



*	Motor: walks assisted/unassisted axempts more frequent.

*	Communication: may say 1—3 meaningful words (varies).
*	Social: imitates routines (brushes hair, waves).



Week 42



*	Cognitive: follows simple instructions without gestures sometimes.
*	Feeding: drinks from a cup with decreasing spill.


Week 43


*	Motor: climbs onto low furniture; enjoys pushing toys.

*	Communication: gestures + single words used together (point + sound).



Week 44 (-:t1months)


*	Social: shows preference for celain people and toys; may test boundaries.

*	Cognitive: explores problem-solving (figures out how to get toy out of container).




Weeks 45-52 (-12 months)



By 12 months many babies pull to stand, cruise, take first independent steps, and say “mama/dada” specifically plus 1—3 other words; strong social/emotional attachments.
 
Week 45-48 (=11-12 months)



*	Motor: many babies pull up, cruise, and take independent steps; improves stooping & recovering.

*	Fine motor: well-developed pincer grasp; can transfer objects hand-to-hand easily.
*	Communication: says "mama" and "dada" specifically; may say 1—3 other words; understands simple instructions.


Week 49-52 (approaching and at 12 months)



*	Motor: walks independently (range -9—15 months typical); climbs on low furniture.
*	Communication: uses gestures, imitates words, may follow simple one-step directions; says several
single words.

*	Social/Cognitive: shows strong attachment to caregivers, plays simple interactive games, demonstrates early problem solving (opens box to get toy).
*	Feeding: aRempts to self-feed with spoon; drinks from cup; tries more textures.



Sources: CDC 1-year milestones, AAP HealthyChildren 12-month page, UNICEF. ([CDC][2])

•	Does not respond to loud sounds or doesn't turn to voices by 3 months.
•	Doesn't track moving objects by 2 months or does not smile back by 3 months.

•	Doesn't roll by 6 months, or doesn't sit with help by 6 months.
